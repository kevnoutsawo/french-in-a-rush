Get French-ready
with these essential words

This is a very minimal example of what my work will look like when I am hired as a French Teaching Assistant for Ikenna's highly awaited upcoming language learning app.

You can get comfortable enough to successfully handle basic conversations in a language like French with a few hundred words. In this article, we will quickly explore some of the most useful words for a beginner in French.

Pronouns are everywhere, they help us designate people or things and refer to them indirectly when needed. Since most sentences use them, let's start there.

Pronouns

Personal pronouns

French English
Je I
Tu “You”: singular and informal
Il He
Elle She
On One
Nous We
Vous “You”: plural, or singular and formal
Ils They: used for a group of people or things of masculine or mixed gender
Elles They: used for groups of people or things of feminine gender only

Demonstrative pronouns

French English
Celui The one : Masculine
Celle The one : Feminine
Ceux Those : used for a group of people or things of masculine or mixed gender
Celle Those : used for groups of people or things of feminine gender only
ça, cela That : Something far in space or time
ce, ceci This : Something close in space or time

Possesive pronouns

French English
Mon My : For singular masculine nouns
Ma My : For singular feminine nouns
Mes My : For plural nouns
Ton Your : For singular masculine nouns, Informal
Ta Your : For singular feminine nouns, Informal
Tes Your : For plural nouns, Informal
Son his, her, or their : For singular masculine nouns
Sa his, her, or their : For singular feminine nouns
Ses his, her, or their : For plural nouns
Notre Our : Singular form
Nos Our : Plural form
Votre Your: Singular and formal
Vous Your: Plural and formal
Leur Their: Singular
Leurs Their: Plural

Other core french words

Question words

French English
Comment How (is also often used as a substitute to "Pardon?" as a polite way to ask people to repeat what they just said)
Qui Who
Quand When
Où Where
Pourquoi Why (Not to confuse with "Pour quoi?" which means "for what" and is written in two words. It is pronounced the same so you will rely on context to know if the person talks about a cause (why) or a goal (for what)
Quoi What

Main answers to close-ended questions

French English
Oui Yes
Non No
Je ne sais pas I don't know

Some essential French verbs

être - To Be

French English
je suis I am
tu es you are
il/elle/on est he/she/one is
nous sommes we are
vous êtes you are (formal, plural)
ils/elles sont they are

Avoir - To have

French English
j'ai I have
tu as you have
il/elle/on a he/she/one has
nous avons we have
vous avez you have (formal, plural)
ils/elles ont they have

Aller - To go

French English
je vais I go
tu vas you go
il/elle/on va he/she/one goes
nous allons we go
vous allez you go(formal, plural)
ils/elles vont they go

Faire - To do

French English
je fais I do/make
tu fais you do/make
il/elle/on fait he/she/one does/makes
nous faisons we do/make
vous faites you do/make(formal, plural)
ils/elles font they do/make

Useful sentences

Pronunciation French English

Comment vous appelez-vous? What is your name?
Je suis ravi(e) de faire votre connaissance. I am delighted to meet you.
Je parle un peu français I speak a little French
Je suis là pour les vacances I’m here for vacation
Où est l’hôtel? Where is the hotel?
Pouvez-vous m’appeler un taxi, s’il vous plaît? Can you call me a taxi, please?

Je vous doit combien? How much do I owe you?
Je ne comprends pas I don’t understand
Parlez plus lentement, s’il vous plaît Speak a little slower, please
